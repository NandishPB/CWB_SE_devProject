Protect what you earn: The Ultimate Guide To Financial Cybesecurity

Protecting your finances online means staying alert and adoptng smart digital habits. Use strong passwords, enable multi-factor authentication,and watch out for phishing scams to keep your money and pesonal data safe from cyber threats.

Signs there might be a problem with your account include:

  • unfamiliar or unauthorized transactions, money movement or deposits;
  • missing funds or securities;
  • incorrect or unauthorized updates to account information, such as a change of address, email or phone number;
  • unexpected notifications indicating a change to your account that you didn't request;
  • missing account statements, confirmations or other financial documents;
  • unfamiliar accounts or creditors on your credit report;

Scammers, hackers, and identity thieves are looking to steal your personal information - and your money. But there are steps you can take to protect yourself, like keeping your computer software up-to-date and giving out personal information only when you have a good reason.

Steps to Protect Yourself

  1. Use Security Software That Updates Automatically
  2. Treat Your Personal Information Like Cash
  3. Check Out Companies to Find out Who You're Really Dealing With
  4. Give Personal Information Over Encrypted Websites Only
  5. Protect Your Passwords
  6. Back Up Your Files

Be Proactive: Safeguard Your Accounts

Watch What You Click

Aviod clicking suspicious links, emails, or attachments. Instead, directly visit your financial institution's official website or app to verify communiction. Engaging with fraudulent messages can install malware and expose your data.

Use Strong Passwords

Do not share passwords and never leave them unprotected. Use a different strong password for each account and change them regularly. Consider using a trusted password manager to generate and securely store complex passwords.

Enable Multifactor Authentication

MFA significantly reduces account takeover risks by requiring two or more verification methods, such as a password plus a text code, fingerprint, facial recognition, or voice authentication.

Maintain Device Security

Install antivirus, anti-spam, and anti-spyware software and enable automatic updates and patching. Apply device security updates immediately. Research cloud providers carefully, ensure they use encryption and MFA, and review their security policies and service terms before storing financial data.

Use Your Own Device

Avoid public computers, which may capture passwords and PINs. If necessary, clear cache, history, and temporary files after use. Protect mobile devices and financial apps with strong passwords, passphrases, or biometrics.

Browse Carefully

Access financial accounts only on secure websites that begin with “https” and display a padlock icon. Avoid multitasking across sites while logged in to prevent session stealing. Always click “log out” instead of simply closing the browser.

Be Cyber Safe When Using Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi networks may have reduced security, and fake networks may mimic trusted names. Avoid accessing financial accounts on unsecured networks. Use encrypted home Wi-Fi protected with strong passwords.

Review All Correspondence From Your Financial Institutions

Carefully review account activity and monthly statements as soon as they are available. Keep your contact information updated and report any errors or unauthorized activity immediately.

Additional Information

If you access your accounts online or through apps, most financial institutions offer optional text and email alerts or device notifications whenever a change is made to your account, or for any online transaction.
